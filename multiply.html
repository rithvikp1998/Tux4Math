Intro

The following method can be used to multiply any two numbers. But the method is most effective when both the numbers are closer to a base. If you don't know or don't remember what a base is, then go through our tutorial here.

Step 1

Once you have two numbers, let the numbers be 'n1' and 'n2'. Find a base that is closer to both the numbers. Find the deviations of both the numbers from the base and let the deviations 'd1' and 'd2'. Proceed to the next step with these numbers.

Step 2

Now, we have two numbers and two deviations. Now take deep breaths and follow the instructions slowly. Take either 'n1+d1' or 'n2+d2'. You will realise both are the same. To this add the same number of zeroes that are in your base. Let the number be 'x'. Now multiply 'd1' and 'd2' and add the product to the 'x'. If you didn't get it, don't worry. Just go through the following example.

Example 1
Let the numbers be 7 and 8, i.e n1 = 7 and n2 = 8. The base that is closer to both the numbers is 10. The deviation of 7 from 10 is -3 i.e.d1 = -3. The deviation of 8 from 10 is -2, i.e. d2 = -2.

n1 = 7
n2 = 8
Base = 10
d1 = -3
d2 = -2
n1+d2 = 5 = n2+d1 :)
The number of zeroes in our base is 1. So, let's add one zero to the sum n1+d2(or n2+d1) which means x = 50
d1 * d2 = 6
The final answer is 50 + 6 i.e. 56!

Math Behind 'Quick Multiplication'

Let the numbers be 'n1' and 'n2'. Let the base be 'b'. Let the deviations be 'd1' and 'd2'.

Deviation = Number - Base i.e. Number = Base + Deviation
n1 = b + d1
n2 = b + d2

n1 * n2 = (b + d1) * (b + d2)
             = b*b + b*d1 + b*d2 + d1*d2
             = b * (b + d1 + d2) + d1 * d2

b + d1 + d2 = n1 + d2 = n2 + d1

Therefore, n1 * n2 = b * (n1 + d2) + d1 * d2 = b * (n2 + d1) + d1 * d2.

We assumed x = b * (n1 +d2) = b * (n2 + d1)
We also assumed y = d1 * d2
